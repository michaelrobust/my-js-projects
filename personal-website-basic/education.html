My Education

Stevens Institute of Technology

My favorite class was CS-546 Web Programming, where I learned how to build full-stack web applications using modern technologies like Node.js, Express, and MongoDB.

I'll never forget the time during finals week when I spent 12 hours straight debugging a single line of code, only to realize I had forgotten a semicolon. It taught me the importance of taking breaks and having fresh eyes look at problems.

Lincoln High School

My favorite class was AP Computer Science, which introduced me to programming for the first time and sparked my passion for software development.

During my junior year, I organized a coding club that grew from 5 members to over 30 students. We built a mobile app for the school cafeteria menu that the administration actually implemented, which made me realize the real-world impact programming could have.

Roosevelt Middle School

My favorite class was Mathematics, where I discovered my love for logical problem-solving and analytical thinking that later helped me in programming.

I remember being selected to represent our school in the district math competition. Even though I didn't win, the experience of working under pressure and presenting my solutions to judges gave me confidence that I still carry today.

Washington Elementary School

My favorite class was Library Science, where I learned research skills and discovered my love for learning new things independently.

Mrs. Johnson, our librarian, let me help organize the computer lab and taught me basic typing skills. I was so excited to use the computers that I would volunteer to stay after school just to practice typing games and explore educational software.
